Research Paper 1 | iCOB 2025 & COBRP 2025

Impact of the US-China Trade War on Asia Pacific's Stock Market Performance: Challenges and Sustainable Approaches

Studies on the impact of the US-China trade war on financial markets over time have been conducted at many levels. Moving beyond historical 2018–2020 periods, this paper shifts attention to the 2025 period, highlighting how ongoing Trade Policy Uncertainty (TPU) continues to influence 12 Asia-Pacific financial markets and sectoral dynamics inside Vietnam.

The research focuses on evaluating how trade conflict shocks influence abnormal returns, volatility behavior, and spillover effects across APAC stock exchanges and 10 industry groups within Vietnam’s VN-Index in 2025. It links financial resilience with United Nations SDG 8 (Decent Work and Economic Growth), offering a forward-looking perspective on sustaining growth amid prolonged trade tensions.

Mô hình hóa Biến động và Tác động Shock (ARMAX-GARCH-M & DCC)

The paper adopts a advanced volatility modeling framework:

  • Risk-Return Trade-off: Draws on the GARCH-in-Mean (GARCH-M) specification, introducing a conditional volatility risk premium term into the mean equation to capture how investors price assets under policy stress.
  • ARMAX-GARCH-M (TPU): Integrates Trade Policy Uncertainty (TPU) as an exogenous variance regressor to measure its direct impact on conditional variance behavior.
  • DCC-GARCH: Employs Dynamic Conditional Correlation (DCC) to trace the time-varying connectedness, identifying which economies act as primary transmitters or receivers of volatility.

Academic and Practical Insights

  • Regional Transmission: Empirically maps the volatility connectedness of the 12 APAC markets, identifying Hong Kong as a key intermediary in absorbing and transmitting volatility between US, China, and APAC markets.
  • Sectoral Sensitivity (Vietnam): Pinpoints Vietnamese industries with higher or lower sensitivity to tariff shocks, supporting portfolios in risk mitigation.
  • SDG 8 Integration: Demonstrates that maintaining financial market stability through active risk pricing directly supports long-term economic growth and decent work opportunities in trade-exposed emerging markets.
Research Paper 2 | CELG 2025 Presentation

Stock Market Reactions to U.S. Fed Rate Cuts: A Comparative Event Study of the U.S. and Vietnam, with an Analysis of Spillovers between the two Markets and within Vietnam's Sectors (2019 - 2020 & 2024 - 2025)

This research evaluates and compares U.S. and Vietnamese stock market reactions to conventional Federal Reserve policy rate cuts. It assesses the short-term market reaction magnitude and compares the responses of 10 industry groups within each country.

To capture evolving investor behavior, the comparative analysis is conducted across two distinct macroeconomic cycles: the pre-pandemic normal phase (2019–2020) and the post-pandemic recovery/tightening turnaround phase (2024–2025). The study also maps volatility transmission, investigating cross-border spillovers from the U.S. to Vietnam and intra-market spillovers between domestic sectors.

Event Study & VAR Spillover Framework

The paper utilizes a integrated methodological framework:

  • Event Study: Computes Abnormal Returns (AR) and Cumulative Abnormal Returns (CAR) around Fed rate cut announcement windows to evaluate market efficiency and short-term announcement sensitivity.
  • Diebold & Yilmaz (2012) Spillover Index: Applies a Vector Autoregression (VAR) framework with generalized forecast-error variance decompositions (GFEVD) to build a connectedness matrix.
  • Cross-Border & Intra-Market Connectedness: Captures both international transmission (US to Vietnam sectors) and domestic sector-to-sector volatility propagation.

Main Results & Policy Relevance

  • Macro Comparisons: Establishes that while unexpected rate cuts trigger general liquidity relief, responses vary heavily between pre-pandemic and post-pandemic cycles, reflecting changes in market risk-aversion.
  • Transmission Roles: Identifies which specific industries act as net risk transmitters versus net receivers of volatility inside HOSE (Vietnam) and U.S. markets.
  • Practical Application: Guides retail and institutional investors in developing sectoral hedging strategies, and assists state regulators in maintaining macro-prudential stability against external currency/monetary shocks (aligned with SDG 8).
